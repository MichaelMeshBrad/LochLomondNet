What is a Radio Net?

A radio net is a regular on-air gathering of amateur radio operators. It provides a structured way for stations to check in, chat, share information, and practice good operating skills.

This net takes place every Sunday evening and is open to licensed amateur radio operators using AllStar, EchoLink, and connected RF nodes.

New to amateur radio? The Radio Society of Great Britain (RSGB) is the UK’s national organisation and a great place to learn more and get started.

Note: This is not CB radio. Amateur radio requires a licence issued by Ofcom, and operators use personal callsigns. It offers far greater range, flexibility, and technical capability than CB.
